# ListingIgnite – X/Twitter Engagement Strategy

This file covers how to engage on X/Twitter as @ListingIgnite during the
pre-launch phase. Read alongside BRAND-VOICE.md and TWITTER.md before
writing any replies, reposts, or engagement copy.

---

## Account Status

- Handle: @ListingIgnite

- X Premium: Active (blue checkmark live)
- Goal: Build visibility and credibility through genuine engagement
  before broadcasting more content

## The Core Mindset

Engagement is not marketing. It is relationship building. Every reply
should add something real to the conversation. The goal right now is
to become a recognizable, trusted presence in the real estate and
PropTech space on X - not to promote ListingIgnite in every interaction.

Promotion comes later. Presence comes first.

---

## The Mental Filter - What to Engage With

Before replying, liking, or reposting anything, run it through this filter:

### REPLY when:
- A real estate agent complains about or mentions time spent on listing
  content, marketing, MLS descriptions, social posts, or admin work
- Someone asks a genuine question about real estate marketing that you
  can answer helpfully without pitching ListingIgnite
- A PropTech or SaaS founder posts something about building in public
  that you can relate to authentically
- A real estate media account posts something you have a genuine
  informed take on
- Someone replies to your own posts

### LIKE when:
- The post is relevant to real estate, PropTech, or SaaS
- You agree with the take or find it genuinely useful
- It is from an account in your target audience (agents, brokers,
  PropTech founders)
- You want to show up in their notifications without saying anything yet

### REPOST when:
- The content is genuinely useful to real estate agents
- It aligns with your brand values (efficiency, professionalism,
  results-focused)
- It is from a credible account in the real estate or PropTech space
- You would be comfortable with anyone associating this repost with
  ListingIgnite

### NEVER engage with:
- Political content of any kind
- Controversial or divisive topics unrelated to real estate or SaaS
- Complaints or negativity about specific companies or people
- Anything that could embarrass a professional brand
- Spam, bots, or accounts with suspicious activity
- Posts that are purely self-promotional from other accounts
  (liking/reposting these adds no value)

## Reply Voice Rules

These supplement BRAND-VOICE.md for the specific context of replies:

### No em dashes
Never use "—" in any reply. Restructure the sentence instead.

### No exclamation points
Per BRAND-VOICE.md. Even in casual replies.

### No sycophantic openers
Never start a reply with "Great post!", "Love this!", "So true!",
"This is amazing!" or any variation. These read as hollow and
algorithm-gaming. Get straight to the point.

### Short is better
Replies should be 1-3 sentences maximum in most cases. Long replies
look like lectures. Short replies that add real value get noticed.

### First person, active voice
"I spent two years watching agents redo this from scratch every listing."
Not "It has been observed that agents often find themselves repeating..."

### No pitching in replies
Do not mention ListingIgnite by name in replies to other people's posts
unless someone directly asks what you are building. Let your profile
do that work. If someone is curious, they will click through.

### Exception - when someone asks directly
If someone asks "what are you building?" or "what does your tool do?"
in a reply, answer clearly and concisely. This is the one time you
can mention the product. Keep it one sentence, no hype.

Example: "A tool that generates a full listing marketing kit in seconds -
MLS copy, social posts, email blast, video script - from one property input."

## Reply Frameworks by Situation

### Agent complains about time spent on listing content
Acknowledge the pain specifically, add something real, do not pitch.

Example post: "Just spent 3 hours writing MLS copy and social posts
for one listing. There has to be a better way."

Good reply: "Three hours is about right when you do it properly.
Most agents I've talked to say social posts take longest because
each platform needs a different tone. The MLS copy at least has
a formula."

Bad reply: "I built something that solves this! Check out
listingignite.com"

### Agent asks a real estate marketing question
Answer it genuinely and completely. No strings attached.

### PropTech or SaaS founder posts a build-in-public update
Engage peer to peer. Ask a real question or share a related experience
from building ListingIgnite. This community is supportive and reciprocal.

### Real estate media posts industry news
Add a perspective relevant to agents or the listing process if you
have one. Skip it if you don't have something real to say.

### Someone posts a hot take you disagree with
You can push back respectfully if it is relevant to your space.
Thoughtful disagreement gets more engagement than agreement.
Keep it professional and specific - disagree with the idea,
not the person.

## Repost Strategy

Repost sparingly - maximum 2-3 reposts per day. A feed that is
mostly reposts looks like you have nothing original to say.

### Quote repost vs. straight repost
- Straight repost: use when the content stands perfectly on its own
  and your audience would benefit from seeing it
- Quote repost: use when you have something genuine to add that
  provides additional context or a related take

### Quote repost format
Keep it short. One or two sentences max. Your added commentary
should make the repost worth seeing, not just repeat what the
original said.

## What Success Looks Like Right Now

In the first 2 weeks, do not measure success by follower count.
Measure it by:

- Number of genuine replies sent per day (target: 5-10)
- Quality of accounts engaging back with you
- Whether agents are clicking through to your profile
- Whether anyone asks what you are building

Follower growth follows engagement quality, not the other way around.

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

- Replying to every single post from one account in a short period
  (looks like stalking)
- Engaging only with huge accounts (replies get buried, nobody sees them)
- Engaging only with tiny accounts (no visibility benefit)
- Copy-pasting the same reply to similar posts (X can detect this)
- Engaging in bursts of 50+ actions then going silent for days
  (inconsistency hurts algorithmic reach)

The sweet spot is mid-size accounts (1,000 to 50,000 followers) in
your target space where your reply has a chance of being seen but
the account is credible enough to matter.

---

## Daily Engagement Rhythm (Suggested)

- 10-15 minutes in the morning: like and reply to overnight activity
  in your niche
- 10-15 minutes midday: engage with fresh posts from target accounts
- 10-15 minutes evening: check replies to your own posts, engage back

Total: 30-45 minutes per day. Sustainable and consistent beats
sporadic and intense every time.
